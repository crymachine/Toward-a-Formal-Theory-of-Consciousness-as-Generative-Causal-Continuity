# 17. Hierarchy of Dynamic Systems

The theory allows building a non-binary hierarchy.

A purely passive system, such as a rock warming in the sun, has a physical state and minimal material memory, but low agency, low self-modeling, and low functional integration. Its degree of consciousness would be close to zero.

A bacterium has internal states, biochemical feedback, minimal temporal memory, and the ability to orient movement relative to environmental gradients. Its degree of consciousness, if not zero, would be extremely low.

A plant has biochemical integration, light orientation, response to stress, and physiological memory. It could have a higher degree of dynamic organization, while still lacking complex phenomenal experience comparable to that of animals.

An animal with a centralized nervous system has memory, integrated perception, active movement, learning, pain, attention, and predictive behavior. Its degree of consciousness would be significantly greater.

A human being has, in addition to these factors, language, metacognition, autobiographical memory, symbolic reflection, theory of mind, planning, and self-narration. Its degree of consciousness is high, especially due to the contribution of `R`.
