# 20. Falsification Conditions

The theory could be falsified or strongly weakened in the following cases.

## Falsification 1

A robust demonstration of phenomenal consciousness in a system lacking persistent memory and temporal continuity.

## Falsification 2

A robust demonstration of phenomenal consciousness in a system that is completely decomposable into causally independent parts.

## Falsification 3

A demonstration that agency and self-modeling do not affect consciousness correlates in any way, all else equal in information processing.

## Falsification 4

The identification of an empirical consciousness indicator that predicts better than the parameters `M`, `Φ`, `T`, `A`, `R`, `B` and renders these parameters superfluous.

## Falsification 5

A demonstration that systems with high estimated values of `M`, `Φ`, `T`, `A`, `R`, `B` are systematically devoid of any independent indicator of experience, even when compared with biological conscious systems.

## Falsification 6

In the aesthetic domain, a systematic demonstration that the degree of indeterminacy/loss of the work has no effect on experiential resonance, emotional response, or the variety of internal reconstructions, once cultural familiarity and perceptual salience are controlled.

These conditions are difficult to apply because the central problem remains the lack of direct access to subjective experience. However, making failure conditions explicit is essential to avoid the model becoming unfalsifiable.
