# 23. Conclusion

The proposed theory interprets consciousness as the self-integrated continuity of a delimitable dynamic system.

Consciousness would not be simply information, nor simple computation, nor simple behavior. It would be the degree to which a system:

- preserves persistent memory;
- causally integrates its states;
- maintains temporal continuity;
- acts to modify its future inputs;
- builds models of itself and the environment;
- possesses sufficiently coherent dynamic boundaries.

In synthetic form:

```math
C(S,t) = f(M, \Phi, T, A, R, B)
```

The most original part of the theory is the link between consciousness and representational irreducibility. Experience does not coincide with what is communicated, measured, or simulated. Every representation leaves a residue. Consciousness can be interpreted as the continuous internal process that generates that residue when observed from the outside.

The aesthetic extension shows that the residue is not only a limit. In art, informational loss can become a coupling condition. The artwork does not transfer the artist's conscious state integrally, but encodes a structure capable of generating resonance in the viewer. The epsilon is not only loss: it can become tolerance, adaptation, and reconstruction.

Applied to the universe, the theory suggests a cautious conclusion: we cannot scientifically assert that the universe is a unitary consciousness; however, we can formulate the hypothesis that local consciousnesses are processes through which the universe-system produces partial representations of itself.

The theory remains open. Its value does not lie in providing a definitive answer, but in transforming a philosophical intuition into a criticizable, formalizable, and potentially testable structure.
