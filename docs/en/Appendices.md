# Appendix A — Operational Glossary

**Consciousness (`C`)**  
Degree of self-integrated continuity of a dynamic system capable of memory, integration, agency, and self-modeling.

**Memory (`M`)**  
Causal persistence of the system's past in its future dynamics.

**Causal Integration (`Φ`)**  
Degree to which the system produces effects as a whole not reducible to the sum of its causally independent parts.

**Temporal Continuity (`T`)**  
Degree of coherence between successive states of the system.

**Agency (`A`)**  
The system's ability to modify the distribution of its future inputs as a function of its internal state.

**Self-Modeling (`R`)**  
The system's ability to represent, estimate, or control its internal state.

**Boundary (`B`)**  
Degree of causal delimitation of the system relative to the environment.

**Representational Loss (`ε`)**  
The gap between a phenomenon and its finite representation.

**Work (`O`)**  
External artifact that partially encodes an internal state and can act as a mediator between conscious systems.

**Resonance (`ρ`)**  
The internal transition of the viewer toward a state compatible with the phenomenological region encoded by the work.

**Artistic Coupling Coefficient (`K_art`)**  
The capacity of a work to generate experiential coupling between imperfectly aligned conscious states.
