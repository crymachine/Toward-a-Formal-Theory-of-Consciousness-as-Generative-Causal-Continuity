# 22. Limits of the Proposal

The theory has important limitations.

The first limit is the problem of subjective experience. Even if memory, integration, continuity, agency, and self-modeling were measured perfectly, the question would remain whether these properties really produce “something it is like.”

The second limit is the risk of over-attribution. If consciousness is a gradient, we must avoid attributing significant consciousness to any physical system simply because it has state or causality.

The third limit is the difficulty of measuring `Φ` in real complex systems. Even IIT encounters significant computational difficulties in calculating informational integration.

The fourth limit is dependence on subjective reports in human beings. The science of consciousness often has to infer experience from behavior, language, and neural correlates.

The fifth limit concerns the cosmological hypothesis. Applying the theory to the universe is conceptually legitimate but empirically fragile. Without specific predictions, the hypothesis of a conscious universe remains a metaphysical possibility, not a scientific conclusion.

The sixth limit concerns aesthetics. Artistic response depends on culture, learning, autobiographical memory, bodily state, expectations, context, and individual differences. The coefficient `K_art` cannot be reduced to a single objective property of the work.
