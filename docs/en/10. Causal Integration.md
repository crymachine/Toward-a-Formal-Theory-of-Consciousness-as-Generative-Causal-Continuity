# 10. Causal Integration

The variable `Φ` indicates the degree to which a system produces effects as a whole rather than as the simple sum of independent parts. This idea is close to, but not identical with, Integrated Information Theory.

A general formulation of causal integration can be:

```math
\Phi(S) = \min_{P \in \mathcal{P}} D\left(P(S_{t+1}\mid S_t), \prod_{k} P(S^k_{t+1}\mid S^k_t)\right)
```

where:

- `P(S_{t+1}|S_t)` describes the causal dynamics of the whole system;
- `S^k` are the parts of the system under a possible partition;
- `\mathcal{P}` is the set of partitions considered;
- `D` is a divergence between the whole-system dynamics and the decomposed dynamics.

If the behavior of the whole system is well approximated by the product of independent parts, then `Φ` is low. If instead the decomposition destroys essential causal properties, `Φ` is high.

This variable helps avoid a common mistake: confusing amounts of information with consciousness. A database can contain a lot of information without being conscious, because its contents do not necessarily form an integrated, recurrent, self-modeling, and agentic dynamic.
