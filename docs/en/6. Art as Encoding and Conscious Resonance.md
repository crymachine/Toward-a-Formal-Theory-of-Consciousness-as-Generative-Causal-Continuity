# 6. Art as Encoding and Conscious Resonance

The analysis of ordinary communication can be extended to art. When we listen to a song, read a poem, or observe a photograph, we may feel “touched.” This phenomenon can be interpreted as a particular case of coupling between conscious states mediated by an external informational structure.

The thesis is not that the artwork literally transfers a portion of the artist's consciousness. That would be too strong and not operative. A more rigorous formulation is:

> Art is an external, partial, and structured encoding of an internal conscious state, capable of coupling with the internal state of another conscious system and inducing in it an experiential reconstruction that is not identical, but resonant.

Let `S_a(t)` be the artist's conscious state at the time of producing the work. The work can be described as a partial encoding:

```math
O = Enc(S_a(t))
```

The viewer does not receive `S_a(t)` directly. They receive `O`, that is, an external structure composed of sound, rhythm, word, color, shape, image, gesture, or temporal sequence. This structure is decoded through their internal state:

```math
S_f(t+1) = Dec(O, S_f(t), M_f, K_f, B_f)
```

where:

- `S_f(t)` is the viewer's internal state before exposure to the artwork;
- `M_f` is their memory;
- `K_f` is their cultural and knowledge context;
- `B_f` is their bodily, emotional and biographical state.

The outcome is not identity between artist and viewer:

```math
S_f(t+1) \neq S_a(t)
```

but partial resonance:

```math
S_f(t+1) \sim S_a(t)
```

The artwork increases the probability that the viewer will generate an internal state belonging to a phenomenological region compatible with the artist's original state:

```math
P(S_f(t+1) \in \mathcal{R}_a \mid O) > P(S_f(t) \in \mathcal{R}_a)
```

where `\mathcal{R}_a` indicates the space of states resonant with the conscious state encoded in the artwork.

In this sense, the work does not contain the artist's consciousness as a transferable content. It contains a generative structure capable of orienting the internal dynamics of another conscious system.
