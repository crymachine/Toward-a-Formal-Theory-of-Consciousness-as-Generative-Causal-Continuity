# Toward a Formal Theory of Consciousness as Generative Causal Continuity

This repository contains the theoretical documents, conceptual models, formal notes, experimental explorations, and software artifacts related to the development of a preliminary formal theory of consciousness as **Generative Causal Continuity**.

The central aim of the project is to investigate whether consciousness can be described not as a binary property, nor as the mere possession or transmission of information, but as a degree of dynamic organization in a system capable of preserving internal continuity, causally integrating its states, modeling itself and its environment, and actively influencing the distribution of its future inputs.

## Project Status

This project is currently in an early research and formalization phase.

The theory presented here should be understood as a developing research framework rather than a finalized model. Its purpose is to define concepts, introduce operational variables, formulate testable hypotheses, compare the framework with existing theories of consciousness, and explore possible experimental and computational implementations.

## Core Idea

The working hypothesis of this project is that consciousness does not coincide with data that can be represented, transmitted, simulated, or externally observed.

Instead, consciousness is approached as a form of **internal causal continuity**: a system-specific process through which successive internal states remain dynamically connected while preserving a form of identity across time.

In this view, observation, measurement, communication, symbolic representation, and artistic expression necessarily introduce structural loss. What is transmitted is not the lived phenomenon itself, but a partial encoding of it through signals, symbols, images, sounds, behaviors, or other external forms.

This loss is not treated only as a defect. In some contexts, especially aesthetic and artistic ones, it may act as a generative tolerance: a partial gap that allows resonance, reinterpretation, coupling, and transformation between conscious systems.

## Research Goals

The main goals of this repository are:

- to develop a formal theory of consciousness as generative causal continuity;
- to define operational variables related to memory, causal integration, agency, self-modeling, and representational loss;
- to formulate conceptual and mathematical models that can be refined over time;
- to compare the theory with established frameworks such as Integrated Information Theory, Global Workspace Theory, active inference, and related approaches;
- to derive predictions and possible falsification criteria;
- to explore computational experiments, simulations, and empirical scenarios;
- to investigate the relationship between consciousness, representation, aesthetics, art, and experiential resonance;
- to examine limiting cases, including the universe considered as a global system.

## Conceptual Scope

The theory currently focuses on the following conceptual dimensions:

### Causal Continuity

The persistence of an internally connected trajectory across successive system states.

### Causal Integration

The degree to which the system’s internal states are mutually dependent and cannot be fully decomposed into independent sub-processes without loss of explanatory structure.

### Memory and Identity

The capacity of the system to preserve traces of prior states in a way that contributes to its current organization and future evolution.

### Self-Modeling

The ability of the system to maintain internal models of itself, its boundaries, its state transitions, and its relation to the environment.

### Agency

The capacity of the system to influence the future distribution of its own inputs through action, selection, attention, prediction, or environmental modification.

### Representational Loss

The structural loss introduced when an internal conscious process is externally represented, measured, described, communicated, or encoded.

### Aesthetic Resonance

The coupling between an external artifact, such as an artwork, and the internal state of a conscious system, producing partial reconstruction, resonance, transformation, or experiential alignment.

## Relation to Existing Theories

This project does not assume that existing theories of consciousness are wrong or irrelevant. Instead, it aims to define a complementary formal framework that can be compared with them.

Relevant points of contact include:

- **Integrated Information Theory**, especially regarding causal integration and irreducibility;
- **Global Workspace Theory**, especially regarding access, broadcasting, and global availability of information;
- **Active Inference**, especially regarding self-modeling, prediction, action, and control of future inputs;
- **Dynamical Systems Theory**, especially regarding state transitions, attractors, continuity, and identity over time;
- **Cosmopsychism and panpsychist-adjacent frameworks**, only as limiting philosophical cases, not as assumptions of the theory.

The framework does not claim that the universe is necessarily a unified conscious subject. A more cautious hypothesis is that local conscious systems may be interpreted as processes through which the universe generates partial representations of itself.
